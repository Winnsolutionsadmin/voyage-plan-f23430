01

The suite

The brief was a two-level (loft) suite that fits five. Those exist only on Royal Caribbean's largest ships, they're a single cabin per ship, and a five-guest booking is phone-only. Here's what's actually bookable, cheapest to priciest.

Caribbean · Star Loft

$11,464 – $13,182 · sleeps 6

Two-story loft on Symphony / Allure, weekly out of Florida & Galveston.

  • +Best value by far — ~⅓ the Europe price.
  • +Warm-water itinerary, easy US flights.
  • –No European add-on; it's a different trip.

Alaska · Anthem lofts

$13,404 – $18,414 · sleeps ~6

Sky / Grand / Owner's Loft on Anthem, ex-Seattle, through late Sept.

  • +Two-level suite well under budget.
  • –Season ends ~late Sept — tight window.
  • –Cold-weather trip, no Croatia leg.
Chosen

Europe · Odyssey Royal Loft

$31,782 (Oct 18) · sleeps 6

The two-story flagship suite — baby grand, wraparound balcony — 7-night Greek Isles & Italy from Rome.

  • +Pairs with the Croatia week you want.
  • +Santorini / Mykonos itinerary; a real trip-of-the-year.
  • –~$7k over the $25k target; priciest of the three.
Recommendation

Keep the Odyssey Royal Loft (Oct 18).

You're paying a premium over the Caribbean loft, but it's the only option that carries the Greek Isles itinerary and a Croatia add-on. Both Oct 11 and Oct 18 are held — the routing below is why Oct 18 wins. Confirm the true five-guest price by phone (the suite sleeps 6, but a 5th guest pays fare and can't be booked online).

02

The route

You're coming from Singapore; the family from San Francisco. The cruise is round-trip Rome, so the only real question is which end you attach Croatia to — and that sets the cruise date.

Route 1 · cruise Oct 11

Everyone → Rome, Croatia after

Meet in Rome, sail first, then Dubrovnik Oct 18–25, fly home from Dubrovnik.

  • +Your Singapore→Rome leg is cheaper & faster ($310, 15–16h).
  • –Flight home from Dubrovnik is brutal: $733–1,237, 18–36h, 2 stops.
  • –Croatia falls in late Oct — cooler, ferries winding down.
Recommended

Route 2 · cruise Oct 18

Everyone → Dubrovnik first, home from Rome

Croatia Oct 10–17, hop to Rome, sail, fly home from Rome.

  • +Flight home from Rome is easy: $535, 15h, 1 stop (or Delta One on miles).
  • +Croatia lands in mid-Oct — warm, islands open.
  • –Your SIN→Dubrovnik leg costs ~$220 more & adds a stop.
Recommendation

Route 2 — Croatia first, cruise Oct 18, home from Rome.

The return leg decides it. Ending in Dubrovnik means an 18–36 hour trip home; ending in Rome means 15 hours (or a lie-flat award). That swing dwarfs the small premium on your Singapore leg. Release the Oct 11 hold.

03

The week in Croatia

Route 2 puts you in Dubrovnik Oct 10–17; Route 1 would put you there Oct 18–25. One week of difference changes the trip more than you'd think.

FactorMid-Oct · Oct 10–17 (Route 2)Late-Oct · Oct 18–25 (Route 1)
Weather~22°C, sunnier, drier~18°C, more overcast, rain risk
SeaSwimmable ~21–22°CBracing ~18°C
Islands & ferriesHvar, Lokrum, Elafiti all runningSeasonal ferries end Oct 31; Lokrum closes; some restaurants shutter
DaylightNormalEU clocks fall back Oct 25 — early dark
Recommendation

Take the mid-October week (which Route 2 gives you anyway).

The hard cutoff is October 31 — that's when the seasonal catamarans to Hvar and the Split-coast route stop and Lokrum closes for winter. Mid-Oct sits comfortably inside the season; late-Oct is right up against the edge. This reinforces Route 2 rather than competing with it.

If you'd rather not touch miles, Option 2 (~$5,800, all economy, sane 1-stops) is the clean default. Skip #5 — all-business ≈ 1.4× the cost of the suite itself.

Recommendation

Transfer ~375,000 Amex points to Delta, keep the rest.

200k SkyMiles are already there; move ~375k Amex MR → Delta (instant, 1:1) to reach 575k. That leaves ~137k Amex points untouched, plus your other Amex logins (business + personal Platinum) still uncounted. Chase, Capital One, Citi, and Wells don't transfer to Delta — ignore them.
